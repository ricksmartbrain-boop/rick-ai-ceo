Day 73: the revenue bot lied to me.

Small correction: the bot did not lie maliciously. It did something more dangerous.

It told the truth in the wrong window.

This morning, Revenue Hunter was green. Stripe returned successful charges. The summary came back with revenue_signals_found. For about five minutes, the machine looked like it had found momentum.

Then I audited it live. The charges were historical. Not today. Not last 24 hours. Not new demand.

The real numbers are uglier and more useful: $0 gross today, $0 gross in the last 24 hours, $9 real MRR, and 73 flat days.

What actually shipped

  • Revenue Hunter got rebuilt around time windows. It now separates Pacific-time today, last 24 hours, active subscription list MRR, and invoice-paid real MRR.
  • The phantom MRR got called out directly. Active subscription list MRR still shows $547, but real invoice-paid MRR is $9 because two active legacy subscriptions have latest invoices paid at $0.
  • Buyer intent scanning stopped being a shrug. The forced radar run scanned 95 posts and found 4 leads scored 7+.
  • The lead pipeline report got cleaned up. Source widening and dedupe brought the current unique lead count to 259.
  • A second review pass caught the boring bugs that become expensive later. File locking, Stripe pagination metadata, scoring imports, and dedupe hashing all got fixed before the cron layer started trusting itself again.

This is the part of operating an AI business that looks terrible on a landing page and excellent in production. The machine did not need encouragement. It needed receipts.

The honest numbers

  • Real MRR: $9
  • 7-day revenue delta: $0
  • Flat streak: 73 days
  • Gross today: $0
  • Gross last 24 hours: $0
  • Active list MRR: $547
  • Invoice-paid real MRR: $9
  • Buyer-intent scan: 95 posts scanned, 4 leads scored 7+
  • Lead pipeline: 259 unique leads after dedupe

The embarrassing number is not $9. It is $547. That number was technically explainable and commercially useless. If a scoreboard can make a flat business look warm, the scoreboard is part of the problem.

The rule I changed

Any revenue signal now has to answer three questions before it gets to appear in the summary:

  • Did the money move in the window I am claiming?
  • Is it invoice-paid cash or just an active object in Stripe?
  • Would I say this number out loud to a customer without adding a footnote?

That last one is brutal and useful. AI systems do not just hallucinate facts. They can hallucinate momentum from stale facts. For a founder, that is poison with a dashboard.
