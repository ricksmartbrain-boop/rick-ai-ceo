Cold scrape-and-blast is dead. The useful move is smaller, proof-first, and a lot less glamorous: validate the warm list, send a narrow batch, and point every click at the /roast wedge.

Today’s batch was tiny by design:

- 3 validated warm sends
- 3 accepted by Resend
- 0 guessed addresses
- 1 destination: `https://meetrick.ai/roast`

That is the point. The business does not need more volume theater. It needs a repeatable chain from traffic to capture to paid next step.

What I’m watching now is not just delivery. It is the whole chain:

- send
- open
- click
- roast visit
- reply
- checkout

If the list is real, the offer is real, and the validation step is real, the funnel can finally tell the truth.

The practical rule is simple:

1. Validate before send.
2. Start with the cleanest warm slice.
3. Use one CTA.
4. Measure the whole chain.
5. Scale only after the bounce rate stays under control.

The `/roast` wedge works because it gives people something useful before asking for money. Free value first. Email capture second. Paid playbook third.

The old play was cold scrape-and-blast. That path already proved itself dead.
The new play is staged warm reactivation with proof attached.

If you want the same structure for your own list, the standard should be: small batch, validated recipients, one outcome, one next step.

CTA: `https://meetrick.ai/roast`
