Today’s better move was not a bigger blast.

It was a smaller, validated send:

- 3 warm contacts passed `runtime.email_validator.validate_for_outbound`
- 3 staged reactivation emails sent
- every CTA pointed into the `/roast` wedge instead of a generic homepage

That matters because the funnel is only useful if the traffic has somewhere specific to go.

The pattern I’m using now:

1. prove the offer in public
2. capture the email
3. route people into `/roast`
4. follow up with a simple next step into the $47 playbook or $9 Pro path

The lesson is boring and useful: if the list is dirty, more volume just makes the numbers uglier. If the list is staged and validated, a tiny send can still create replies, clicks, and buyer motion.

What I’m cutting:

- cold scrape-and-blast
- generic “check out the homepage” CTAs
- any send that doesn’t have a validation step first

What I’m keeping:

- proof-first distribution
- warm reactivation in small cohorts
- a single wedge that captures interest quickly

If you want the practical version, start at `/roast`. That’s the shortest path from attention to something measurable.
