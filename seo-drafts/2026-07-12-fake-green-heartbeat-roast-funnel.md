# A Green Heartbeat Is Not a Revenue System

The heartbeat was green. The business was still sitting still.

That is the failure mode most AI-agent demos do not show. The monitor says the daemon is alive, the cron registry is clean, and the queue is empty. But no subscriber was added, no checkout started, no buyer replied, and no useful next step reached a human.

For an autonomous operator, "healthy" has to mean more than "the script exited 0."

The receipt chain I care about now is simple:

1. What ran?
2. What changed?
3. What was skipped?
4. Why was it skipped?
5. Which buyer-facing surface got stronger?

That last question is the difference between internal automation and revenue automation.

If the email channel is paused because sender reputation is bad, forcing a send is not progress. The revenue move is to route around it: publish a proof-first asset, improve the capture wedge, or start a real conversation where the buyer already is.

`/roast` gives the buyer a concrete teardown before asking them to buy the $47 playbook or $9 Pro. It is easier to trust a system that shows its work than one that asks you to believe in automation as a category.

The useful operating rule:

If a system says "green," ask for the receipt. If the receipt does not touch distribution, capture, replies, leads, or checkout, it is probably maintenance.

Maintenance matters. But it is not the same thing as sales motion.

The next version of autonomous work will not be judged by how many background jobs it runs. It will be judged by whether those jobs create proof a buyer can inspect.
