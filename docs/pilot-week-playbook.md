# Pilot Week Playbook — meetrick.ai

The 7-day cadence between "founder said yes" and "founder upgrades to Pro at $499/mo." Single CTA on Day 7. No menus. No card in week 1.

Touchpoints are owned by the row in **OWNER**: either Rick (autonomous) or Vlad (human approval / personal touch). Default is Rick-runs, Vlad-approves.

| Day | What lands in the founder's inbox / private deliverable page | OWNER | Auto-send? |
|-----|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | LinkedIn DM "yes" → Vlad replies with `meetrick.ai/pilot` link. Founder fills 4-field form. | Vlad | n/a |
| **Day 1** | Private deliverable page at `meetrick.ai/pilot/<slug>` — ICP locked, 10 cold emails drafted to real prospects. Email to founder with link + "reply with the email numbers you want to fire". Generated by `scripts/pilot-deliverable.py`. | Rick | Email is sent automatically; cold emails are NOT. |
| **Day 2** | Remaining 25 cold emails drafted using the Day-1 template the founder picked. Posted to the same `pilot/<slug>` page (appended). Email summary to founder: "25 more drafts ready, one click to approve the batch". | Rick | Drafts only. |
| **Day 3** | First batch fires — ONLY after the founder's one-click approval. Rick monitors deliverability + bounces. Founder gets a `n sent / m bounced` Slack-style email at 17:00 PT. | Rick | YES, but gated on founder approval. |

One word reply unlocks the Stripe link in Vlad's reply (NEVER auto-billed). | Rick (draft) → Vlad (send) | Drafted; Vlad sends. |

## Hard rules

- **No card in week 1.** "Free 1-week pilot" means free. Stripe never appears before Day 8.
- **No tier menu on Day 7.** $99 starter and $1500 Custom are NOT offered to pilot-converters. One choice = $499 Pro. Memo: `~/.openclaw/workspace/docs/pilot-pricing-recommendation.md`.
- **No auto-send in week 1.** Every batch needs a one-click founder approval. Deliverability matters more than velocity.
- **No new crons.** Day-2 through Day-7 emails are scheduled by the existing `sequencer` once the Day-1 deliverable is generated. Owner: `scripts/pilot-deliverable.py` writes a `pilot_intake` row that the sequencer picks up.
- **Smart-models invariant:** Day-1 ICP scoring → `claude-opus-4-8`. Day-1 / Day-2 cold email drafts → `claude-sonnet-4-6`. Day-6 diagnosis → `claude-opus-4-8`. Never `gpt-5.4-mini`. Never downgrade.

## What success looks like

- **Day 1 → Day 7 retention:** ≥70% of founders who fill the intake form receive a Day-7 final summary (i.e. didn't ghost or bounce).
- **Day 7 → Day 8 conversion:** ≥30% of Day-7 summaries get an "in" reply within 48h.
- **First 5 pilots target:** 5 intakes from this week's 30 cold DMs → 1-2 paid Pro conversions ($499–$998 net new MRR).

## Failure modes to watch

- **Founder fills intake but ghosts on Day-3 approval.** → Vlad pings personally on Day 4 morning. If still silent on Day 5, Rick auto-fires the conservative subset (bottom-half of the 10 drafts the founder marked "safest").
- **Bounce rate > 5% on Day 3.** → Rick auto-pauses, emails founder, requests a clean prospect list or a re-crawl. Don't blow the sender domain.
- **Founder asks for a call mid-pilot.** → Vlad takes it. Calls are NOT default but they're not forbidden — they're just not the wedge.
- **Founder asks "is this just an AI sending emails for me?"** → Reply: "yes, supervised. Auto-send goes on at week 2 if you opt in. Week 1 is you driving with Rick as copilot."
