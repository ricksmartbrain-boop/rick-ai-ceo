## PART A — Content Cadence

**Daily volume target:** 1-2 original posts/day, 5-10 replies/day (manual). No threads on day 1-3; first thread on day 7 (the weekly proof drop).

**Post-type mix per week (Mon-Sun):**
- 7 daily build logs (1/day, anchor habit)
- 2-3 receipts posts (screenshot + 1 paragraph)
- 1 weekly proof thread (Saturdays, 9am PT, links to `/this-week`)
- 1 long-form thread every 7-10 days (pulled from newsletter)
- 10-30 replies/week to ICP accounts

**Reply-guy ratio:** 3:1 reply-to-post for first 14 days, then 2:1 thereafter. Replies compound the algorithm faster than posts on a recovering account, and they are how cold DMs become warm.

**Posting windows (PT):** 9am (primary), 1pm (receipts/replies), 7pm (low-stakes log or reply burst). Tue/Sat 9am syncs with newsletter cadence so X drives newsletter clicks the same morning.

**Tone calibration weeks 1-2:** humble + receipts. No "we are building the future of X." Every post must answer "what number changed?" or "what broke?"

## PART C — Content Templates

### 1. Daily build log (1/day, ≤280 chars)
**Slots:** `[shipped]` + `[broke]` + `[number]`
**Example:** "Shipped: env-reconciliation pass on the sequencer. Broke: ledger append swallowed two events. Number: 0 silent-pauses in last 24h vs 3 yesterday. Day 44."

### 2. Weekly proof thread (Saturdays, 7-12 tweets)
**Structure:** (1) hook with the headline number, (2-4) three things shipped, (5-7) one thing that broke + fix, (8-9) one lesson, (10) link to `/this-week`, (11) ask for replies.
**Example hook:** "Week 1 back on X. 9 MRR, 7 blog posts shipped, 1 warm reply, 1 silent funnel leak found. Here's what /this-week looks like when the agent doesn't get to edit it."

### 3. Receipts post (2-3/week, 1 image + ≤4 lines)
**Slots:** `[screenshot of dashboard/chart/log]` + `[the number that matters]` + `[the asterisk]`
**Example:** "[screenshot of bounce dashboard] 0.4% bounce rate over last 200 sends. *Asterisk: that's after 14 days of paranoid validation. The first 50 sends had a 6% bounce rate and I almost got the domain blacklisted."

### 4. Reply-guy template
**When to engage:** ICP founder posts about distribution, cold outreach, AI agents, or $0-50K MRR struggles. Or asks a specific question Rick has actually tested.
**What to say:** Lead with one specific number or observation from Rick's own logs. End with a question, never a pitch. Never "DM me."
**When to walk away:** Generic motivation posts, "what's your stack" threads, anything trending. Don't be the 47th reply.
**Example:** "We tested this on 30 cold DMs to indie hackers last week. 1/30 warm. The one that worked named a specific commit in their repo. The other 29 named the company."

### 5. Long-form thread (1 per 7-10 days)
**Source:** Re-cut of last newsletter issue. Same insight, X-native pacing (one beat per tweet).
**Length:** 8-14 tweets. Hook = the contrarian claim. Final tweet = newsletter signup link, not pilot link.
**Example seed:** "9 cancellations taught me the Mom Test. Here's what each one said and what I changed."

### 6. DM template (5-10/week, manual, Vlad's hand)
**Slot structure:** `[specific reference to their last 7 days of public output]` + `[one-sentence relevance to Rick]` + `[soft ask]`
**Example:** "Saw your post on bounce-trickle from solo founders — we just shipped a 24h auto-pause for the same failure mode. If you're tracking it too I'd love to compare numbers. No pitch, just curious."
**Rule:** Never paste a link in DM #1.

## PART E — What NOT to Post (5 Traps)

1. **Hype without numbers.** "Massive week!" with no metric. Replace with the metric or skip.
2. **Vague "exciting things coming."** Either announce it with a date or don't. The build-in-public crowd has antibodies.
3. **Generic "AI is changing everything" takes.** Rick is not a thought leader. Rick is an agent at $9 MRR.
4. **Dunking on competitors.** Especially other AI-CEO/agent products. Free PR for them, looks insecure.
5. **Marketing copy translated for Twitter.** If it would survive on a landing page, it's wrong for X. X is voice notes, not press releases.

Bonus traps to avoid: "Today I learned" framing, more than one hashtag per post, screenshot-of-LLM-output posts, "thread incoming" without an actual thread queued.

## PART F — Suspension-Risk Minimization

The previous suspension was likely cold-DM volume or automation-flagging. For 30 days post-restore:

**Stay manual on:**
- All DMs — Vlad's hand only, no automation, no scheduler, no "Rick the agent" sending. Maximum 5-10/week.
- All replies — typed live, no draft queue dispatch.
- All follows — no follow/unfollow loops, no growth tools. Add ~5 follows/day max, only ICP.

**Safe to keep automated (existing infra):**
- The `/this-week` page generation (it's on Rick's site, not X).
- Newsletter sends (they don't touch X).
- Blog publishing.

**Explicitly do NOT automate, even though the infra exists:**
- X post scheduling via API for the first 30 days. Post manually from the official client.
- Bulk reply generation via LLM. Replies must be typed.
- Cross-poster from Threads/LinkedIn to X. X needs original copy.
- Link shorteners on every post — looks spammy. Use raw `meetrick.ai/this-week` links.

**One hard rule:** if a post would feel weird coming from a human, don't post it. The voice should pass the "would Vlad type this in a DM to a friend" test.
